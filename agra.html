The Taj Mahal is one of the world's most iconic monuments, commissioned in 1632 by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Built with pure white marble, it's considered the finest example of Mughal architecture, blending elements of Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles.

Agra itself is a city filled with historical wonders, from majestic forts to vibrant bazaars, offering a rich glimpse into India's royal past and architectural brilliance.

Top Places to Visit in Agra

Taj Mahal

Location: Dharmapuri, Agra

A symbol of eternal love and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Agra Fort

Location: Rakabganj, Agra

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and royal residence of the Mughal emperors.

Meh Tab Bagh

Location: Opposite Taj Mahal

A garden complex offering scenic views of the Taj, especially during sunset.

Things to Do in Agra
1. Go beach hopping

One of the most popular and pioneering of them is Soul & Surf, which provides surf and yoga classes, along with accommodations

2. Cruise the palm-fringed backwaters

Experience the serene backwaters aboard a traditional houseboat in Alleppey or Valiyaparamba.

3. Drink in the local spirit

To imbibe the true spirit of Kerala, try toddy, traditiionally called kallu.

4. Savor traditional art forms

Watch Kathakali dance dramas, Theyyam rituals, and Kalarippayat martial arts performances.

5. Relax in Munnar's green hills

The British favored the area in the 19th century as a coller summer escape from the sun-baked plains, and today, it retains its refreshing charm.

Taj Mahal Entry & Visitor Tips
  • Taj Mahal is closed every Friday.
  • Tickets can be bought online or at the gate (foreigners have a separate line).
  • Free water bottles and shoe covers are provided with foreigner tickets.
  • Photography is not allowed inside the mausoleum.
  • Only small bags allowed. No food, tobacco, or pens.
  • Nearby monuments offer discounts if visited the same day.

Getting Around

Private Cabs

Convenient and comfortable for sightseeing. Prebook online or via hotels.

Local Buses

Budget-friendly option to move around Agra, but may be less comfortable.

Auto Rickshaws

Ideal for short distances, remember to always negotiate or insist on meter use.

Walking

Many key attractions are near each other, perfect for a stroll through history.
