Manila

"The Historical Soul of the Philippines"

"Where heritage meets modernity."

Manila, the bustling capital of the Philippines, blends rich Spanish colonial heritage with a vibrant urban culture. From historic walled cities to vibrant street life, it offers a unique Southeast Asian experience.

Whether you’re exploring old churches, strolling by Manila Bay, or diving into the thriving food scene, this city is full of surprises.

Top Places to Visit in Manila

Intramuros

Location: Old Manila

The historic Spanish-era walled city featuring Fort Santiago and centuries-old churches.

Rizal Park

Location: Roxas Boulevard

A large historical park dedicated to national hero José Rizal. A great place for walks and cultural displays.

Manila Ocean Park

Location: Near Rizal Park

A marine-themed park with aquariums, ocean tunnels, and sea lion shows — fun for all ages.

Things to Do in Manila
1. Sunset walk by Manila Bay

Take in a gorgeous tropical sunset from the Baywalk area, followed by street snacks or a chilled coconut drink.

2. Shop and dine at Mall of Asia

One of the largest malls in Asia — enjoy shopping, watch 4D movies, and try Filipino dishes at seaside restaurants.

3. Ride a jeepney

Hop on a colorful jeepney — the classic Filipino mode of transport — for a fun and cheap ride around town.

Entry & Visitor Tips
  • Respect cultural norms, especially in historic or religious areas like churches and monuments.
  • Carry cash for small purchases — many local vendors don’t accept cards.
  • Use Grab app for safe, air-conditioned transportation.
  • Watch out for rush hour traffic — plan trips accordingly!

Getting Around

LRT & MRT

Efficient rail lines connecting Manila's key areas, avoid during peak hours.

Grab & Taxis

Grab is the safest and easiest way to get around the city.

Buses & P2P

Point-to-Point (P2P) buses offer comfy air-conditioned rides to popular malls and airports.

Car Rentals

Available via apps or hotels — great for day trips to nearby Tagaytay or Batangas.
