Beijing

"The Ancient Capital of Modern China"

"Where ancient dynasties meet modern marvels"

Beijing, China's sprawling capital, has history stretching back 3 millennia. Yet it's known as much for its modern architecture as its ancient sites. From the sprawling Forbidden City complex and Temple of Heaven to the contemporary curves of the National Stadium and CCTV Headquarters, Beijing is a city of dramatic contrasts.

As the political and cultural heart of China, Beijing offers visitors an unparalleled look at both imperial history and the nation's rapid modernization. The city's famous hutongs (narrow alleys) reveal traditional courtyard life, while its gleaming skyscrapers showcase China's economic rise.

Top Places to Visit in Beijing

Forbidden City

Location: Dongcheng District

The world's largest palace complex with 980 buildings from Ming and Qing dynasties.

Great Wall

Location: Mutianyu (90 min from city)

Best-preserved section with stunning mountain views and fewer crowds.

Temple of Heaven

Location: Dongcheng District

Imperial complex where emperors prayed for good harvests.

Things to Do in Beijing
1. Walk through the Forbidden City

Explore the vast imperial palace that was off-limits for 500 years, home to 24 emperors.

2. Hike the Great Wall at Mutianyu

Less crowded than Badaling, this restored section offers cable cars and toboggan rides down.

3. Watch locals practice tai chi in Temple of Heaven Park

Morning is best to see retirees dancing, exercising, and playing traditional instruments.

4. Explore the hutongs by rickshaw

Wander narrow alleyways to see traditional courtyard homes and local life.

5. Visit the Summer Palace

Imperial retreat with Kunming Lake, Long Corridor, and Marble Boat.

Getting Around

Metro

Beijing's extensive subway system is clean, efficient and cheap (MYR 2-5 per ride). Signs and announcements are in English.

Taxi/Didi

Taxis are affordable but language can be a barrier. Didi (Chinese Uber) has English app options.

Bike Sharing

Mobike & Ofo bikes are everywhere. Scan QR codes to unlock (need Chinese payment apps).

Aiport Express

Dedicated train line connects to city center in 20 minutes (MYR 15).
