Kerala

"God's Own Country"

"A land of breathtaking landscapes and rich cultural traditions"

Kerala in southwestern India may lack the grand monuments and forts of the north, but it more than makes up for it with its breathtaking landscape. This tiny tropical state along India's Malabar Coast has palm-fringed backwaters, uncrowded beaches, tea- and spice-covered hills in the Western Ghats, and steamy jungles.

It is also rich in history, with temples, mosques and churches that have stood for centuries and a culture that includes outlandish yet profoundly transfixing dance forms, martial arts and Ayurvedic practices.

Top Places to Visit in Kerala

Alleppey Backwaters

Location: Alappuzha

A network of palm-fringed canals best explored by traditional houseboats.

Munnar Tea Gardens

Location: Munnar

Endless rolling hills covered in lush tea plantations in the Western Ghats.

Kovalam Beach

Location: Thiruvananthapuram

Famous crescent-shaped beach with lighthouse and surfing opportunities.

Things to Do in Kerala
1. Go beach hopping

One of the most popular and pioneering of them is Soul & Surf, which provides surf and yoga classes, along with accommodations

2. Cruise the palm-fringed backwaters

Experience the serene backwaters aboard a traditional houseboat in Alleppey or Valiyaparamba.

3. Drink in the local spirit

To imbibe the true spirit of Kerala, try toddy, traditiionally called kallu.

4. Savor traditional art forms

Watch Kathakali dance dramas, Theyyam rituals, and Kalarippayat martial arts performances.

5. Relax in Munnar's green hills

The British favored the area in the 19th century as a coller summer escape from the sun-baked plains, and today, it retains its refreshing charm.

Getting Around

By Air

Kerala has 4 international airports: Kochi (COK), Trivandrum (TRV), Calicut (CCJ), and Kannur (CNN). Direct flights from Middle East, Southeast Asia, and major Indian cities.

By Train

Extensive rail network connects Kerala to all major Indian cities. Important stations include Trivandrum Central, Ernakulam Junction, and Kozhikode.

By Bus

Excellent bus services connect Kerala to neighboring states. KSRTC operates comfortable AC and non-AC buses throughout the state.

By Water

Ferry services operate between islands and along backwaters. Major ports at Kochi and Vizhinjam handle cruise ships & international vessels.
