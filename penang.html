Penang

"The Pearl of the Orient"

"A harmonious blend of heritage, culture, and culinary delights."

Penang, known as the "Pearl of the Orient", blends heritage, culture, and food like no other Malaysian destination. George Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, charms visitors with colonial architecture and colorful street art.

From sandy beaches and lush hills to mouthwatering street food, Penang offers a rich experience that delights every kind of traveler.

Top Places to Visit in Penang

George Town

Location: Penang Island

A UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for street art, old shophouses, and cultural diversity.

Penang Hill

Location: Air Itam

Take a funicular train up for panoramic views, colonial mansions, and a cool escape from the heat.

Kek Lok Si Temple

Location: Air Itam

One of Southeast Asia's largest Buddhist temples, featuring intricate architecture and a towering statue of Kuan Yin.

Things to Do in Penang
1. Explore George Town's street art and heritage

Hunt for famous murals and steel rod sculptures while admiring the well-preserved colonial architecture in this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

2. Indulge in Penang's famous street food

Sample local specialties like Char Kway Teow, Assam Laksa, and Cendol at famous hawker centers like Gurney Drive and New Lane.

3. Attend a Batik or Craft Workshop

Get hands-on with local art at places offering batik painting, rattan weaving, or ceramic making.
