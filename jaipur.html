Jaipur

"The Pink City of India"

"The city where history whispers from every pink wall and palace corridor"

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is a flamboyant city of forts and palaces, vibrant bazaars, and rich cultural traditions. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaipur was India's first planned city, with wide avenues and uniform facades painted pink to welcome the Prince of Wales in 1876, a tradition that continues today.

Part of the Golden Triangle tourist circuit along with Delhi and Agra, Jaipur dazzles visitors with its magnificent Amber Fort, intricate Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), and the astronomical instruments at Jantar Mantar.

Things to Do in Jaipur
1. Explore Amber Fort

This majestic hilltop fort with its mirror-work palaces and elephant rides offers breathtaking views of Maota Lake.

2. Marvel at Hawa Mahal

Jaipur's iconic "Palace of Winds" with its 953 windows allowed royal ladies to observe street life unseen.

3. Visit City Palace

The royal residence features museums with historic artifacts and the stunning Peacock Gate.

4. Get a traste of Rajasthani cuisine

Some of the best thalis can be had at Suvarna Mahal, Smaode Havekli, and Laxmi Misthan Bhandar.

5. Shop in Johari Bazaar

Jaipur's famous jewelry market is perfect for buying precious gems, Kundan and Meenakari work.

Getting Around

By Air

Jaipur International Airport (JAI) is 13 km from the city center, with domestic flights from major Indian cities and international connections.

By Train

Jaipur Junction is well-connected to Delhi (4-5 hrs on Shatabdi Express), Mumbai, Ahmedabad, and other major cities.

By Bus

Comfortable AC and non-AC buses connect Jaipur to Delhi (5-6 hrs), Agra (4-5 hrs), and other Rajasthan cities.

By Car

The Delhi-Jaipur highway (NH48) makes driving convenient (5-6 hrs). Taxis and self-drive cars are available.
