Kyoto

京都

“In Kyoto, you can really experience the spirit of Japan.”

— Prince Charles (now King Charles III)

Once the imperial capital of Japan, Kyoto is home to thousands of temples, traditional teahouses, and serene gardens. With its timeless elegance and rich cultural heritage, Kyoto offers a glimpse into the country's spiritual heart.

From bamboo forests to geisha districts, every step in Kyoto feels like a walk through history and beauty.

Top Places to Visit in Kyoto

Fushimi Inari Shrine

Location: Fushimi Ward, Kyoto

Invites the soul on a sacred journey through endless vermilion torii gates, where each step echoes with ancient prayers and divine presence.

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

Location: Ukyo Ward, Kyoto

A dreamlike sanctuary where emerald stalks rise like ancient guardians, and every rustle of the leaves carries a whisper from another world.

Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)

Location: Kita Ward, Kyoto

Gleams in radiant gold, casting a luminous reflection over its serene pond—an eternal symbol of Kyoto’s gilded beauty and Zen serenity.

More Things to Do in Kyoto
1. Experience a Tea Ceremony

Learn the art of Japanese tea preparation and mindfulness in Gion or Higashiyama.

2. Dress in a Kimono

Stroll around Kyoto in traditional dress — great for photos in historic streets.

3. Explore Gion

The geisha district filled with wooden machiya houses and quiet charm.

4. Visit Nijo Castle

A UNESCO site with gorgeous gardens and “nightingale” floors that chirp as you walk.

5. Take a Philosopher's Path Walk

A tranquil walk lined with cherry blossoms and temples, ideal in spring and fall.

Getting Around

Subway & Buses

Kyoto’s bus system covers all major attractions. The One-Day Bus Pass is a tourist favorite.

Walking

Many areas like Gion, Higashiyama, and Arashiyama are best explored on foot.

Bike Rental

Scenic and convenient for getting around Kyoto’s flatter areas and quiet streets.

JR Lines

Use the JR Pass to reach Kyoto from Tokyo or Osaka and explore nearby towns like Nara or Uji.
