Jeju Island

제주도

"Jeju is Korea’s island of peace, nature, and volcanic beauty."

— UNESCO World Heritage

Jeju Island is a volcanic paradise, famous for its natural beauty, scenic coastlines, lava tubes, and Hallasan Mountain. It's a favorite getaway for both locals and international travelers.

From beach sunsets to forest walks, and from quirky museums to traditional stone walls, Jeju offers relaxation and adventure in equal measure.

Top Places to Visit in Jeju

Hallasan Mountain

Location: Central Jeju

South Korea’s highest peak, rises majestically at the heart of Jeju Island, offering scenic trails through lush forests and breathtaking crater views.

Manjanggul Cave

Location: Gujwa-eup, Jeju-si, Jeju-do

Step into the shadowy depths of the cave, where time stands still and eerie lava formations whisper the ancient secrets of Jeju’s volcanic past.

Seongsan Ilchulbong

Location: Seogwipo-si, Jeju-do

Climb through lush trails and sea-kissed breezes, leading to panoramic views from the crater rim that make it one of Jeju’s most unforgettable sunrise spots.

More Things to Do in Jeju
1. Visit Jeongbang Waterfall

The only waterfall in Korea that flows directly into the sea.

2. Walk Olle Trails

Jeju's famous coastal trails with breathtaking scenery and stone fences.

3. Try Black Pork BBQ

Jeju’s signature pork grilled over charcoal — tender, juicy, and smoky.

4. Visit the Teddy Bear Museum

Charming and fun for all ages — from historical teddy bears to quirky exhibits.

5. Catch Women Divers in Action

Watch traditional “haenyeo” women divers harvest fresh seafood the old-fashioned way.
