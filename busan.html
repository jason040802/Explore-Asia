"Busan is where mountains meet ocean and calm meets energy."

— From the Heart of the Locals

Busan, South Korea’s coastal gem, offers a unique mix of sandy beaches, fresh seafood, and vibrant markets. It's a city where traditional temples sit peacefully beside bustling seaports and contemporary skyscrapers.

Whether you’re enjoying the sea breeze at Haeundae, hiking up to Beomeosa Temple, or browsing through Jagalchi Fish Market, Busan welcomes you with warmth and charm.

Top Places to Visit in Busan

Haeundae Beach

Location: Haeundae-gu, Busan

Busan’s most iconic shoreline, where golden sands, gentle waves, and a lively boardwalk create the perfect seaside escape in South Korea.

Gamcheon Culture Village

Location: Saha-gu, Busan

Reflects Busan’s artistic spirit and resilient history, where once a modest mountainside settlement now thrives as a living canvas of local heritage and creativity.

Jagalchi Market

Location: Jung-gu, Busan

Korea’s largest seafood market, offers an authentic taste of Busan’s coastal culture, where the daily catch comes to life through the calls of seasoned fishmongers.

More Things to Do in Busan
1. Visit Beomeosa Temple

Serene temple nestled in the mountains with peaceful trails and historical beauty.

2. Walk the Oryukdo Skywalk

A thrilling glass-floored walkway with panoramic coastal views.

3. Ride the Songdo Cable Car

Get a bird’s-eye view of Busan’s stunning coastline from above the sea.

4. Explore BIFF Square

A hub for Korean cinema culture, street food, and trendy shops.

5. Sunset at Gwangalli Beach

Enjoy beautiful views of Gwangan Bridge lighting up the night sky.

Getting Around

Busan Metro

Clean, efficient, and connects major attractions like beaches, markets, and stations.

Taxi

Reliable and widely available. Apps like KakaoTaxi are handy for non-Korean speakers.

Buses

Frequent and cover even remote attractions like Beomeosa and Oryukdo.

KTX Train

Travel from Seoul to Busan in just 2.5 hours via high-speed KTX train.
