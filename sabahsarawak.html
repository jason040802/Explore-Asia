Sabah & Sarawak

Sabah and Sarawak, located on Borneo Island, are known for their untamed jungles, indigenous culture, and rich biodiversity. These states offer adventure, nature, and cultural discovery in one package.

From Mount Kinabalu’s peak to Sarawak’s Mulu Caves and orangutan sanctuaries, these East Malaysian gems invite explorers and nature lovers alike.

Top Places to Visit in Sabah

Mount Kinabalu

Location: Kinabalu Park

The tallest mountain in Malaysia, perfect for trekking and nature photography.

Sepilok Orangutan Centre

Location: Sandakan

Observe rescued orangutans being rehabilitated in a natural forest habitat.

Sipadan Island

Location: Celebes Sea

World-famous diving site with vibrant coral reefs and marine life.

Things to Do in Sabah
1. Soak in Poring Hot Springs

Unwind in the mineral-rich hot springs of Poring, surrounded by lush rainforest and near stunning canopy walkways.

2. Snorkeling or Scuba Diving

Explore one of the world’s best diving spots with vibrant coral reefs, sea turtles, and swirling schools of barracuda in Sipadan Island.

3. Take a Wildlife River Cruise

Hop on a boat along the Kinabatangan River to spot proboscis monkeys, pygmy elephants, crocodiles, and hornbills in their natural habitat.




Top Places to Visit in Sarawak

Mulu Caves

Location: Miri

UNESCO World Heritage Site with some of the world’s largest cave systems.

Sarawak Cultural Village

Location: Pantai Damai

Experience the traditions of Sarawak’s ethnic groups in a living museum.

Bako National Park

Location: Kuching

Famous for proboscis monkeys, rainforest hikes, and beach trails.

Things to Do in Sarawak
1. Go Caving and Spelunking

Challenge yourself with caving adventures through vast underground chambers and limestone tunnels deep in the Sarawak wilderness.

2. Trek Through Dense Rainforests

Embark on guided jungle treks to witness rich biodiversity, from exotic flora to rare wildlife like hornbills and flying squirrels.

3. Experience Indigenous Traditions

Take part in cultural activities such as traditional dance, bamboo cooking, and handicraft-making with Sarawak’s native communities.

Travel Tips
  • Light clothing and insect repellent are must-haves for jungle adventures.
  • Respect local customs and tribal traditions when visiting rural areas.
  • Best time to visit is during the dry season (March to October).
  • Domestic flights between Sabah and Sarawak are common and convenient.
