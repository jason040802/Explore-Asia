Xi'an

"Where Ancient Warriors Stand Guard"

"The birthplace of Chinese civilization"

Xi'an, the capital of Shaanxi Province, is one of China's oldest cities and the eastern terminus of the Silk Road. As the ancient capital for 13 dynasties including the Zhou, Qin, Han, and Tang, Xi'an is a living museum of China's glorious past.

The city is most famous for the Terracotta Warriors, but offers much more - from the perfectly preserved city walls to the Muslim Quarter's vibrant food scene. Xi'an represents the cradle of Chinese civilization while embracing its modern role as the cultural and economic center of Northwest China.

Top Places to Visit in Xi'an

Terracotta Army

Location: Lintong District (40 min from city)

The 8,000 life-sized clay soldiers guarding Emperor Qin Shi Huang's tomb for 2,200 years.

Ancient City Wall

Location: City center

The most complete city wall surviving in China, dating back to the Ming Dynasty.

Muslim Quarter

Location: Behind Drum Tower

Vibrant neighborhood with historic mosques and famous Xi'an street food.

Things to Do in Xi'an
1. Marvel at the Terracotta Warriors

Discover the three excavation pits containing thousands of unique clay soldiers, horses, and chariots.

2. Bike the Ancient City Walls

Rent bicycles to ride along the 14km Ming Dynasty wall encircling the old city.

3. Explore the Muslim Quarter at night

Sample Xi'an's famous street food like yangrou paomo (crumbled bread in mutton soup).

4. Visit Shaanxi History Museum

See artifacts from Xi'an's 3,100 years of history, including Tang Dynasty treasures.

5. See the Big Wild Goose Pagoda

Climb this Tang Dynasty pagoda built to house Buddhist scriptures from India.

Getting Around

Metro

Xi'an's metro system has 5 lines covering major attractions (MYR 2-5 per ride). Clean and efficient.

Taxi/Didi

Taxis are affordable but may not speak English. Have your destination written in Chinese.

Tourist Bus

Special tourist buses (like Bus 306) go to Terracotta Warriors from the train station.

Bike Rental

Rent bikes to ride the city walls or explore the Muslim Quarter's pedestrian streets.
