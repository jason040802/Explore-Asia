Chiang Mai

"The Cultural Soul of Northern Thailand."

"Where ancient temples rise amid misty mountains"

Chiang Mai, the cultural capital of northern Thailand, blends serene landscapes with centuries-old traditions. Surrounded by forested hills and dotted with over 300 temples, the city offers a peaceful retreat with a strong spiritual charm.

Whether you're exploring the historic Old City, joining a lantern festival, or sipping coffee in a jungle café, Chiang Mai invites visitors to slow down and connect with its gentle pace and rich heritage.

Top Places to Visit in Chiang Mai

Bua Thong Waterfall

Location: Mae Taeng District

A hidden natural wonder, where mineral-rich waters flow over grippy limestone rocks, letting you walk effortlessly up its cascading, jungle-framed tiers.

Yi Peng Lantern Festival

Location: Mae On District

In the heart of Chiang Mai, the Lantern Festival paints the night sky with floating stars, as whispered wishes rise like glowing prayers into the heavens.

Khun Wang Tunnel

Location: Mae Wang District

A serene mountain haeven, where vibrant cherry blossoms and cool breezes weave through pine-covered hills and tranquil orchards.

Things to Do in Chiang Mai
1. Experience the Lantern Festival (Yi Peng)

Witness thousands of glowing lanterns float into the night sky, creating a breathtaking tapestry of light and hope.

2. Visit Khun Wang Royal Project

Stroll through blooming cherry blossom trees and lush highland farms nestled in the cool, scenic mountains of Chiang Mai.

3. Climb Bua Thong Waterfall (Sticky Waterfall)

Defy gravity as you walk barefoot up the mineral-rich limestone tiers of this unique, non-slippery waterfall deep in the forest.

4. Explore Doi Suthep Temple

Climb the staircase lined with mythical serpents to reach this sacred temple offering sweeping views over Chiang Mai.

5. Wander through the Old City

Discover ancient temples, quaint cafés, and centuries-old walls as you wander the charming streets of Chiang Mai’s historic core.

Getting Around

Songthaews (Red Trucks)

Chiang Mai's iconic red shared taxis follow flexible routes and are an affordable way to get around—just flag one down and tell the driver your destination.

Grab

Grab is widely used in Chiang Mai and offers convenient, app-based rides with clear pricing and English language support.

Bicycles & Scooters

Renting a bicycle or scooter is a popular way to explore the city and its surroundings—just be cautious in traffic and always wear a helmet.

Walking

The Old City is compact and pedestrian-friendly, with temples, cafés, and shops all within walking distance.
